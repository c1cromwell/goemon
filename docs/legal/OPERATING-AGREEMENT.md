## ARTICLE I — FORMATION, NAME, PURPOSE, TERM

**1.1 Formation.** The Company was organized as a Wyoming limited liability company under the
Act. The rights and obligations of the Members are governed by the Act, the Articles of
Organization, and this Agreement. Where this Agreement differs from a non-mandatory provision
of the Act, this Agreement controls (Wyo. Stat. § 17-29-110).

**1.2 Name.** The name of the Company is **Goemon Global Finance, LLC**. The Company may
operate under the brand **"Goemon"** and registered trade names as the Manager directs.

**1.3 Registered agent / office.** As stated in the Articles of Organization. The principal
office is `[• address]`.

**1.4 Purpose.** Any lawful business under the Act. The Company's business is the development
and licensing of **non-custodial financial software and tokenization infrastructure**. In
Phase A the Company does not take custody of customer funds and **is not a bank**; regulated
activity advances deliberately per `CORPORATE-STRUCTURE.md` §6. **⚖**

**1.4.1 "Bank" naming restriction.** "Bank," "banking," and "deposits" are legally restricted terms. Unless
and until the Company (or a subsidiary) holds the requisite charter, the Company and its officers shall **not
represent, market, or hold the Company out as a bank or as offering banking or deposit-taking services**, and
the Manager shall ensure product and marketing copy conforms (`CORPORATE-STRUCTURE.md` §9(#4), §1). **⚖**

**1.5 Term.** Perpetual, until dissolved under Article XI.

**1.6 Title to property.** All Company property is owned by the Company as an entity; no
Member has any individual ownership interest in Company property.

## ARTICLE V — MANAGEMENT

**5.1 Manager-managed.** The Company is **manager-managed**. Except for Reserved Matters
(§5.4), the **Manager** has full and exclusive authority to manage the business and affairs of
the Company and to bind the Company (Wyo. Stat. § 17-29-407(c)).

**5.2 Initial Manager.** **Chad Cromwell** is the initial and sole Manager, and also serves as
**Chief Executive Officer**. The Manager serves until resignation, death, incapacity, or
removal. **Removal or replacement of the Manager is a Reserved Matter** (§5.4) requiring
unanimous Member consent.

**5.3 Authority of the Manager (ordinary course).** Without limiting § 5.1, the Manager may,
in the ordinary course: hire and terminate personnel and contractors; set compensation; enter
contracts and licenses; manage banking and treasury; **issue awards from the reserved Equity
Incentive Plan pool** (Article VIII); maintain books and the cap table; prosecute and protect
IP; and operate the product consistent with the Phase-A compliance posture. This codifies the
operational **"human CEO gate"** reflected in the product architecture (`CLAUDE.md`,
`docs/GOEMON-BRAND.md` §3).

**5.4 Reserved Matters (unanimous Member consent required).** Notwithstanding § 5.1, the
following actions require the **prior written consent of all Members** (while the Members'
default voting rule of unanimity under § 6.2 is in effect):

1. Amending the Articles of Organization or this Agreement.
2. Admitting a new Member or issuing, redeeming, or repurchasing Units **outside the Equity
   Incentive Plan** (issuance of the reserved 900,000 Pool Units as Plan awards is delegated
   to the Manager under Article VIII).
3. Increasing or decreasing authorized Units, or creating a new class/series of Units.
4. Approving or amending the **Equity Incentive Plan** or increasing the reserved Pool above
   900,000 Units (9%).
5. Incurring indebtedness, guaranties, or capital commitments exceeding **`[$•]`** in the
   aggregate, or granting a lien on Company assets (including any encumbrance of the IP).
6. Selling, licensing exclusively, assigning, or otherwise disposing of all or substantially
   all of the Company's assets or its core intellectual property.
7. A merger, conversion, recapitalization, or reorganization — **including the planned
   conversion to a Delaware C-corporation** (Article VII, § 7.6).
8. Dissolving or winding up the Company.
9. Any **related-party transaction** between the Company and a Member, Manager, officer, or
   their affiliate or family member.
10. Making, changing, or revoking a **material tax election** (including an S-corp or
    entity-classification election) or changing the Partnership Representative.
11. Approving distributions other than ordinary-course and tax distributions (§ 4.2–4.3).
12. Removing, replacing, or materially changing the authority or compensation of the Manager;
    commencing or settling material litigation; or filing for bankruptcy.

**5.5 Officers.** The Manager may appoint officers (e.g., CEO/President, Treasurer/CFO,

## ARTICLE VI — VOTING AND MEMBER ACTION

**6.1 Voting power.** Each Member votes in proportion to that Member's Percentage Interest of
issued and outstanding Units (Schedule A). Reserved (unissued) Pool Units do not vote.

**6.2 Default rule — unanimity.** As of the Effective Date and until changed under § 6.4, **all
Member action requires the affirmative vote or written consent of ALL Members.** The Members
have intentionally chosen unanimous decision-making; both Members hold voting Units and no
Company action requiring Member approval may be taken without both Members' agreement.

**6.3 Action by written consent; meetings.** Members may act by written (including electronic)
consent without a meeting. Meetings may be called by the Manager or any Member on `[• ]` days'
notice; notice may be waived. Electronic/remote participation is permitted.

**6.4 Fallback voting rule (effective only if unanimity is later removed).** If the Members
unanimously agree in writing to remove the unanimity requirement of § 6.2 (itself a Reserved
Matter), then thereafter, unless otherwise agreed: (a) **ordinary matters** are decided by
Members holding a **majority (>50%)** of the outstanding Percentage Interests — under the
current cap table this vests ordinary control in **Chad Cromwell (majority Member)**; and (b)
**fundamental matters** (those listed in § 5.4(1)–(8)) require a **supermajority of `[• 75]`%**
of Percentage Interests. This fallback exists so the Company is not permanently exposed to
deadlock if the Members later choose majority governance.

**6.5 Deadlock and buy-sell.** Because two-Member unanimity creates the possibility of
deadlock, if the Members are unable to agree on a Reserved Matter that the Manager reasonably
determines is necessary for the Company's operation, the following process applies, in order:

1. **Good-faith negotiation** — the Members confer for `[• 30]` days.
2. **Mediation** — non-binding mediation under `[• ]` rules, costs shared equally.
3. **Buy-sell ("shotgun") option** — if still unresolved after `[• 60]` days, either Member
   may deliver a notice offering to buy the other's Units at a stated per-Unit price; the
   recipient must, within `[• 30]` days, elect either to **sell** at that price or to **buy**
   the offeror's Units at the same per-Unit price. Closing occurs within `[• 60]` days.

This mechanism is a backstop, not a default exit; the Members intend to govern by consensus.
**⚖ — buy-sell terms (valuation, financing, family considerations) should be tailored with
counsel.**

## ARTICLE VII — TRANSFER RESTRICTIONS

**7.1 General restriction.** No Member may sell, assign, pledge, or otherwise transfer any
Unit or economic interest except as permitted by this Article. Any attempted transfer in
violation is void.

**7.2 Consent.** Except for Permitted Transfers (§ 7.4), a transfer requires the prior written
consent of all other Members (a Reserved Matter), and the transferee becomes a **Member** (as
opposed to a mere economic assignee) only upon such consent and execution of a **Joinder**
(Exhibit A).

**7.3 Right of First Refusal (ROFR).** Before any transfer to a third party, the transferring
Member must offer the Units **first to the Company**, then **to the other Members** pro rata,
on the same terms as the bona fide third-party offer, with `[• 30]` days to exercise. Units
not taken up may then be transferred to the third party on no more favorable terms within
`[• 90]` days, subject to § 7.2.

**7.4 Permitted Transfers.** A Member may transfer Units, without ROFR but subject to Joinder
and continued application of this Agreement, to: (a) a revocable trust or estate-planning
vehicle for the Member's benefit or immediate family; (b) on death, to the Member's estate or
heirs; provided the transferee takes subject to all restrictions, and **voting rights pass
only with the Manager-/Member-consented admission as a Member**. **⚖ estate-planning tailoring
(e.g., the father/son ownership).**

**7.5 Tag-along / drag-along.** (a) **Tag-along:** if a Member proposes to sell Units to a
third party, the other Members may participate pro rata on the same terms. (b) **Drag-along:**
if Members holding `[• ]`% of Percentage Interests (and, while unanimity is in effect, **all**
Members) approve a sale of the Company, the remaining Members shall participate on the same
terms. **⚖**

**7.6 Planned Delaware C-corporation conversion and holdco/opco structure.** The Members acknowledge the
strategy (`CORPORATE-STRUCTURE.md` §2.2) to **convert the Company to a Delaware C-corporation** upon the
first institutional financing or the standing-up of a regulated subsidiary. Each Member agrees to cooperate
in good faith with such conversion (a Reserved Matter) and to exchange Units for equivalent shares of the
successor corporation in proportion to Percentage Interests, with outstanding Plan awards converting as
provided in the Equity Incentive Plan. The Members further acknowledge that, at or after conversion, the
successor may adopt a **holding-company / operating-subsidiary** structure — the parent holding the IP, cap
table, and brand, with regulated activity (money transmission, RWA issuance, transfer-agent) ring-fenced in
separate licensed subsidiaries (`CORPORATE-STRUCTURE.md` §2.2) — so that a license never contaminates the
Company's core software IP. **⚖ counsel to structure the conversion and any subsidiary.**

## ARTICLE XI — DISSOLUTION AND WINDING UP

**11.1 Dissolution events.** The Company dissolves only upon (a) the unanimous written consent
of the Members (a Reserved Matter), or (b) entry of a decree of judicial dissolution under the
Act. Death, withdrawal, or bankruptcy of a Member does **not** dissolve the Company; the
remaining Member(s) may continue the business.

**11.2 Winding up.** On dissolution, the Manager (or a liquidator) winds up: collects assets,
pays creditors (including Member loans), sets aside reserves, and distributes the remainder to
the Members in accordance with positive capital account balances / Percentage Interests
(Wyo. Stat. § 17-29-708). **⚖ CPA on liquidation waterfall.**

**11.3 Articles of dissolution.** The Company files articles of dissolution with the Wyoming
SOS when winding up is complete.
